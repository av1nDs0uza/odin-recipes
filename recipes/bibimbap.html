Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a delicious Korean dish that consists of rice topped with a variety of vegetables, protein, a fried egg, and a flavorful gochujang (Korean red pepper paste) sauce. Here's a simple recipe for Bibimbap

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 1 cup julienned carrots
  • 1 cup sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 cup thinly sliced spinach, blanched
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched
  • 1 cup thinly sliced cucumber
  • 4 fried eggs
  • Sesame oil (for drizzling)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. Prepare each vegetable separately. Sauté carrots, mushrooms, and spinach with a bit of sesame oil until just tender. Blanch bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 minutes. Slice the cucumber.
  3. For the Gochujang sauce, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, rice vinegar, and minced garlic in a bowl. Adjust the seasoning to your liking.
  4. Assemble the Bibimbap: Divide the cooked rice among serving bowls. Arrange the sautéed vegetables, bean sprouts, and cucumber on top of the rice.
  5. Fry an egg for each serving and place it on top of the vegetables.
  6. Drizzle the Gochujang sauce over the Bibimbap, and finish with a drizzle of sesame oil and a sprinkle of sesame seeds.
  7. To eat, mix all the ingredients together, breaking the egg yolk, and enjoy the flavors!
